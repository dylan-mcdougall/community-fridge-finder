What is a Community Fridge?

The origin of community refrigerators dates back to the early 2010s, when grassroots activists and compassionate individuals sought to address food waste and food insecurity in their local communities. These public fridges, often found in urban areas, serve as a communal space where residents can donate surplus food and those in need can access fresh, nutritious items free of charge. The primary purpose of these refrigerators is to foster a sense of solidarity and communal responsibility, as well as to provide a sustainable solution to hunger and food waste. Over the years, community refrigerators have had a significant impact on many lives, not only alleviating hunger for vulnerable populations but also promoting a culture of sharing, environmental consciousness, and social cohesion. As they continue to grow in popularity, these fridges have the potential to further empower communities and create a more equitable and sustainable food system.
